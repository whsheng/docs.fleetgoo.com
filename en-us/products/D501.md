# D501 Product Manual

> The D501 IoT vehicle recorder is a high-performance automotive-grade device designed for fleet management and vehicle monitoring. It integrates advanced ADAS/DMS algorithms, supports multi-channel cloud video storage, 4G/5G communication, and centimeter-level high-precision positioning, providing excellent safety and operational efficiency for various commercial vehicle applications.

## 1. Product Overview

### 1.1 Product Introduction

The D501 IoT vehicle recorder is designed to meet rigorous demands across industries. It adopts professional high-performance automotive-grade image processing chips and the Linux operating system, ensuring stable operation in industrial environments from -20°C to 70°C. It integrates advanced Driver Assistance Systems (ADAS) and Driver Monitoring Systems (DMS). Utilizing AI variable frame rate technology, the D501 supports comprehensive cloud storage of multi-channel video. It also features 4G/5G full network communication and RTK centimeter-level high-precision positioning.

### 1.2 Core Features

- Supports 2K + dual 1080p video streams
- Automotive-grade chip, industrial-grade operating temperature range from -20°C to 70°C
- AI variable frame rate video cloud storage technology for comprehensive multi-channel video cloud storage
- 4G/5G full network communication
- Beidou/GPS/RTK centimeter-level high-precision positioning
- Integrated ADAS/DMS/BSD algorithms
- Supports remote and automatic ADAS calibration, real-time fault tracking, remote parameter configuration, etc.
- Complies with Ministry standards 794, 808, and related protocol standards
- Supports 9-36V wide voltage input for vehicles, with complete circuit protection (undervoltage, short circuit, reverse connection)

## 3. Installation Instructions

To ensure smooth, efficient, and complete installation, please follow these steps:

- Determine the best wiring point, specifically locating the vehicle fuse box and a suitable grounding point
- Determine the ideal installation location for the D501 recorder and other cameras
- Plan the wiring strategy: integrate with the vehicle's existing wiring or choose a separate concealed wiring route
- Start wiring from the driving recorder installation position based on the above conditions
- After wiring is complete and power-on is confirmed, send the installation pictures and relevant information to backend personnel (if applicable) for data validation. After confirmation, reassemble the vehicle decorative panels, securely install the driving recorder, and complete the installation

### 3.1 Installation Steps

**Installation Precautions:**

- Ensure the installation is secure and waterproof. Avoid high-temperature areas and magnetic interference sources (such as车载 CD players, audio speakers, vehicle computers,车载 radios)
- Ensure the installation position on the windshield is within the wiper range to maintain a clear view, even on rainy days. It should be placed near the rearview mirror for the best view
- Do not touch the lens with your fingers, as grease will leave stains, causing blurry video or image distortion
- During installation, ensure all plug interfaces are correctly and securely connected. For added protection, wrap the connections with electrical tape to prevent water ingress, oxidation, and accidental disconnection. Ensure wiring is concealed to avoid blocking the driver's line of sight and maintain aesthetics

#### Step 1: Insert Cards

Insert the TF card and SIM card into the device, then secure the card cover.

#### Step 4: Secure Front Camera

After setting the front camera angle, secure it by tightening the screws under its cover plate. This prevents vibration or accidental adjustment of the lens direction during vehicle driving.

![Secure Front Camera](../images/d501-camera-securing.png)
*Figure 10: Secure front camera*

#### Step 5: DMS Camera Installation

The DMS camera installation position should follow these principles:

- **Installation Location:** It is recommended to install on the center console or instrument panel
- **Installation Angle and Distance:** Ensure the driver is within ±30° in front of the camera. The recommended angle should be as small as possible. The recommended distance between the camera and the driver's face is 60-120cm, ideally about 80cm
- **Centered Facing:** Ensure the driver's face is centered in the DMS camera view (can be confirmed via mobile APP)
- **No Obstruction:** Ensure the DMS camera does not block the driver's line of sight or interfere with driving
- **Clear View:** Ensure no objects (such as the steering wheel) block the line of sight between the DMS camera and the driver's face
- **Level Alignment:** The DMS camera should be installed horizontally and must not be tilted
- **Optimal Angle:** Under the above conditions, the smaller the deviation angle between the DMS camera and the driver's face, the better. Ideally, it should point directly at the driver

### 3.2 Wiring Instructions

Route the power cable to the fuse box. Connect the ACC wire and constant power wire to the corresponding fuse slots. Connect the GND wire directly to a suitable grounding point (such as a metal bolt on the vehicle chassis).

![Wiring Diagram](../images/d501-wiring-diagram.png)
*Figure 12: Wiring diagram*

| No. | Wire Color | Description |
| :-- | :-- | :-- |
| 1 | Yellow | Power positive (B+) |
| 2 | Black | Power negative (GND) |
| 3 | Red | ACC (Accessory/Ignition switch control) |

**Wiring Precautions:**

- Remove the vehicle's original corresponding fuse and replace it with the power cable's fuse
- The device is usually equipped with a 15A fuse. Do not replace it with a fuse lower than 15A
- The ACC wire (red) controls the device's sleep/wake status. Do not connect the ACC wire to constant power
- The constant power wire connected to the positive (B+) should maintain at least 12V when the ignition is off

## 5. FAQ

| Problem/Fault | Solution |
| :---- | :---- |
| Device falls off | Ensure the car glass is thoroughly cleaned and the 3M tape is firmly pressed. If necessary, replace the 3M tape. |
| Recorder does not power on | Please confirm that the main power cable (such as BMW interface) is inserted firmly, the power cable is connected correctly, and ensure the external power supply is working normally. |
| Video recording does not start after power-on | First ensure the TF card is correctly inserted. If not, please reinsert the TF card after the device is powered off. If these steps fail, please format the TF card or replace it with a new TF card. |
| Recording terminates abnormally | Please format the TF card or replace it with a TF card that meets the requirements (Class 10 or higher). |
| Blurry video image | Please ensure there are no stains on the vehicle windshield.<br>Please ensure there are no stains or obstructions on the recorder camera lens. |
| Recorder unresponsive | Please disconnect the power and restart the recorder. Alternatively, press the device's reset button to automatically restart the recorder. |
| Terminal offline | Check if the SIM card is out of credit; if so, please contact the network operator to settle the bill.<br>Check if the SIM card is in good contact: reinsert the SIM card.<br>If the vehicle is in an area with weak signal, such as an underground parking lot or tunnel, please move to an area with better signal reception. |
| Terminal not positioning | Ensure the antenna connection interface is inserted firmly.<br>Check if the antenna surface is facing up: readjust the antenna position.<br>If the vehicle is in an underground parking lot, tunnel, or other area with no signal, please drive out of the area. |
